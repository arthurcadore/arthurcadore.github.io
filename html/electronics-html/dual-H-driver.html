Dual 'H' DC Motor Driver
Tuesday - 02/08/2022
In some DC applications we need to feed a load (circuit, component or object) in way that allow us to change circuts polarity. The circuit presented in this topic can provide this operation.
First of all, the circuit was assembled in TBJ transistors for testing polarity and load, in sequence, the same circuit can supply more load by addition of relays in circuit that can provide much more power.
In the image below, is presented the circuit use to drive loads with TBJ transistors.
In "control" input, we feed the transistors gates in way to block the current in one way, and free it in other way. The power supply terminals are connected in ground and in the source of TBJ transistors.
Once the circuit is made with TBJ, we can added relays to it to provide more power. In the circuit below, two transistors are connected to relay's inductor to select the operation mode of the circuit. In this case, we only need two transistors instead of four, because the relays can opperate in two states.
The transistor's drain feeds the relays and the power supply is connected into relay's "select pin", like is shows below:
In the top part of the image presented, was added optocoplers to circut to isolate the control signals to the high power part of the circuit, this components transmit the signal with light, so, electronic pulses can't go back and damage the control circuit.
In relay's inductor was also added "flyback" diodes to avoid high voltages spikes provided by current interruption in circut when the control signals stops.
